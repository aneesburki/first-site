How does the Internet work?

Internet works no different than two devices connected to each other through a wire, and the user tries to transfer files from one device to another. Internet is just the connection of billions of computers through wires around the world to share information to each other in the form of websites, images, videos, and so on, through primarily a device identification process (known as IP Address) and a set of rules (such as the Word Wide Web).

Programming Foundamentals Simplified

What exactly is programming?

Programming is about completing a task by a computer by giving it extremely precise instructions. Computers are incredibly dumb, so if the instructions are not 100% precise, then the computer won’t be able to understand your code, and your task will never be completed. A practical example of this can be an unintelligent friend. If you give unclear instructions to your unintelligent friend to complete a task, then he won't be able to complete the task.

Why do programming languages exist?

We also need to translate our instructions into the language of the computer, and the language of any computer is binary language. Otherwise, it wouldn’t understand its task, and the task would never be completed. Think of giving your friend instructions to complete a task in English, but he only speaks Chinese. He wouldn’t be able to understand what we were saying, so the task would never get completed unless the instructions were translated into his language. It’s possible to learn the language of a Chinese friend and then give him instructions in his language, but on the other hand, it’s impossible to learn the binary language of the computer and then give it instructions about a particular task. That’s why; we have programming languages that help as your translator friend between programmers and computers.

What types of programming languages are there?

Programming languages are categorized on the basis of the functions and secondly, the levels of a language.
On the basis of functions, some languages, such as Python and Java, are used for general purposes. On the other hand, Languages such as HTML and CSS are used for specific tasks, such as Web Development.
On the basis of levels, there are two levels of programming language, i.e., Low and high languages. Assembly, C, and so on are examples of low-level languages, and on the other hand, Java, Python, and so on are high-level languages.
The code of Low-Level Languages is closer in appearance to machine code, hence why low-level languages are more difficult than high-level languages.
